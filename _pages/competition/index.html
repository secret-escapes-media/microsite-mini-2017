Your journey begins in...

Inverness

Land in Inverness, the gateway to the Highlands. Your MINI Countryman will be waiting for you on arrival. You may wish to hit the road right away, and who can blame you, your drive is going to be epic. Should you wish to explore however, you’ll find a city that retains its market town feel. On the banks of the river Ness, fine restaurants abound, making this a perfect pit-stop before heading into the wilderness.

Loch Ness

As you leave the city, you’ll trace the road that lines the length of Loch Ness. Breathtaking views over the loch and up the the crags await, with ample opportunity to pull over and bask in the glory of the views. Be sure to look out for the romantic ruin of Urquhart castle, and if you fancy a giggle you can always check out the Nessieland museum in Drumnadrochit. Failing that, head out on this stunning road and see if you can catch the monster peeping above the shimmering waters.

The middle bit…

As you leave the idylls of Loch Ness, the road veers north, and the landscape starts to undulate with jutting peaks on either side. Tarmac stretches run ahead undisturbed, offering sublime driving in near isolation, mile after mile. As you glide past your third loch and near the Isle of Skye, the castle of Eilean Donan juts out into the loch, framed by steep crags and shimmering waters. A perfect spot for a break, and to take in the unique Highland panorama.

Isle of Skye

This stunning isle only improves on the incredible drive thus far embarked upon. Dramatic peaks fall to the coast, as the road twists between loch and crag with ease. As Scotland’s third biggest tourist destination, Skye is famed for its stunning landscape, within which you’ll find a host of cosy pubs, sleepy villages and fine restaurants. It makes for the perfect stop-off after a long day driving on some of the best roads in the UK. You’ll be bedding down [HOTEL TBC]

After a good night’s sleep, head to Uig to catch the ferry to Tarbert.

Outer Hebrides

As you enter Tarbert, you approach a remote collection of islands that splinter outward into the Atlantic. Rugged valleys, crystal pools and dreamy coastline awaits, begging to be explored.

Welcome to

Òran na Mara

Your stay at Őran na Mara typifies the breathtaking panorama of the islands, and the seclusion and privacy granted at such a wonderfully remote getaway. The holiday home is graced with unique design that takes full advantage of the incredible views this beachside property affords. Floor to ceiling windows allow near full panorama of this sublime location, and cosy contemporary design tempts you to spend days indoors – but the open road awaits, and the island call for intrepid exploration.
